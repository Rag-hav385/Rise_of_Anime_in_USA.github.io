Anime Ascendant: The Pandemic's Impact on Anime Consumption and Merchandising in America

Introduction

Anime's surge in the USA during the pandemic showcases its resilience and cultural significance. This article delves into its unprecedented growth.

Anime & Manga: Pandemic Trends in America

In 2020, amidst the pandemic, anime interest in the USA saw a significant surge, contrasting with stable manga interest. The sudden rise, credited to streaming platforms and popular series, reshaped entertainment consumption.

Streaming Success: Pandemic-Driven Anime Resurgence

During the pandemic, anime series like "Jujutsu Kaisen" and "Attack on Titan" experienced significant spikes in popularity, largely due to their availability on Netflix, which attracted a vast new audience and reinvigorated the genre's appeal.

Merchandising Meteoric Rise: Streaming's Impact on Anime Markets

The U.S. anime merchandising market, projected to grow at a 16.1% CAGR from 2023 to 2030, has been buoyed by the pandemic-driven surge in OTT platform viewership. Platforms like Netflix have expanded their anime offerings, significantly influencing market dynamics and consumer purchasing behaviors.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the pandemic has undeniably acted as a catalyst for the anime industry in the U.S., significantly bolstering the anime merchandising market through enhanced OTT platform offerings. The sharp rise in anime consumption has fostered collaborations between merchandisers and anime creators, propelling a vibrant market expansion sustained by a dedicated fan base. As streaming giants like Netflix continue to broaden their anime libraries, they further secure anime's position as a key player in mainstream entertainment.
